# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Independent consultants, small studios, and solo operators who need to capture billable time without managing a heavyweight operations system. They use SkyTime during the workday, often while switching between clients, projects, and admin tasks, then return later to review a clean timesheet for billing, payroll, or records.

## Product Purpose

SkyTime is a simple time tracking workspace for starting a timer, assigning work to projects and tasks, receiving optional browser reminders, organizing lightweight task progress, and exporting polished timesheets by month, financial year, annual period, or custom date range. Success means the user can answer "what did I work on, for whom, and for how long?" with minimal effort.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, practical. The product should feel trustworthy and focused, closer to Toggl Track, Linear, Stripe, and Raycast than a decorative SaaS template. Copy should be direct and spare.

## Anti-references

Avoid purple gradients, generic AI-style dashboards, decorative glass panels, oversized marketing heroes, playful novelty UI, dense enterprise admin clutter, and template content that does not belong to time tracking. The interface should not feel like a landing page wrapped around a half-built app.

## Design Principles

Start with the timer. The fastest action on the screen should be capturing the current work session.

Keep records legible. Time entries, projects, totals, and exports should read clearly under review.

Make nudges optional. Reminders should help users build a habit without becoming nagging or blocking.

Use familiar product patterns. Navigation, forms, task boards, tables, menus, and export controls should behave predictably.

Design for billing confidence. Period filters, totals, and exported files should feel polished enough to send or archive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, focus visibility, and semantic structure. Support reduced motion preferences, avoid color-only state communication, and keep core actions usable on compact laptop and mobile screens.
